Going solar in Lagos does not mean committing to a full building array immediately. For many homes and small offices, a starter kit is the practical first step.

A basic kit is a panel, a charge controller, a battery, and the cabling. It runs lights, phone charging, a router, and sometimes a fan. That alone removes the worst hours of outage.

The advantage of starting small is expansion. Once you know your daily draw, you can add panels and a bigger battery later without throwing away what you bought first. We size starter kits to what you can begin with and tell you exactly what to add next.

If you want the fridge and TV on solar too, that is a larger system with an inverter. We match the inverter rating to the load and the battery capacity to how many hours you need backup.

The mistake is buying a random "kit" online. Match the parts to your load, and the system actually does the job.
